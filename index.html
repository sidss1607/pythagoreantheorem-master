The Pythagorean Theorem



The Pythagorean Theorem is a concept in Geometry describing the relationship between three sides of a right triangle.
It states that the hypotenuse (side opposite the right angle) squared is equal to the the sum of the two legs in squared state.

This Relationship is Put Into an Equation as Follows:
a^2 + b^2 = c^2
